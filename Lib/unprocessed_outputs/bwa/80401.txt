                <td class="label">Abstract/Citation:</td>
                <td>Public Service Act No 30 of 2008 is a direct result of the ratification of three International Labour Organization (ILO) Conventions by the Botswana Government in 1997. Subsequently, national labour laws were amended in 2004 to align them with the ILO Conventions following which Government appointed a Reference Committee comprising some public service unions, representatives from Government appointing authorities, Ministry of Labour and Home Affairs and Attorney Generals' Chambers to merge all employment laws governing public employees.<br><br>Part I - Preliminary<br>Part II - Public Service Structure<br>Part III - Composition of the Public Service<br>Part IV - General Public Service Principles and Standards<br>Part V - Administration of the Public Service<br>Part VI - Appointments to the Public Service<br>Part VII - Termination of Appointments and Retirement<br>Part VIII - Public Officers - General Provisions<br>Part IX - Misconduct and Unsatisfactory Service<br>Part X - Settlement of Disputes<br>Part XI - Freedom of Association for Public Officers<br>Part XII - Industrial Action<br>Part XIII - Collective Bargaining in the Public Sector<br>Part XIV - Offences and Penalties<br>Part XV - Miscellaneous<br></td>
